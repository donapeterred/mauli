In India

In regions like Assam, Tamil Nadu, and Andhra Pradesh, banana fiber was historically used for ropes, mats, and household textiles. Communities discovered that the discarded banana stem, usually thrown away after harvesting fruit, contained strong, durable fibers that could be repurposed. Banana silk (a softer refined version) became part of temple textiles and ceremonial clothing.
